
1. "Dasahi, bhikkhave, dhammehi samannāgato puggalo na sevitabbo."

dasahi: ten
dasa: 10 but auspicious, more meaningful than the number alone
दस - ten

dhammehi: personal qualities ("states" in lesson translation)
dhamma: quality (in this case)
धर्म

sammannagato:
Samannāgata: endowed with, possessed of (adj.)
संपन्न - endowed

puggalo:
puggala: individual / person (m.)
व्यक्ति - person, man

na: negative, no, not

sevitabbo: associate with
sevati (sev): to serve, associate with

--

2. "Katamehi dasahi?"

katamehi: which (instrumental, male, plural)
katama: which / which one (of many)

dasahi: ten

--

3. "Micchādiṭṭhiko hoti, micchāsaṅkappo hoti, micchāvāco hoti, micchākammanto hoti,
micchā-ājīvo hoti, micchāvāyāmo hoti, micchāsati hoti, micchāsamādhi hoti, micchāñāṇī
hoti, micchāvimutti hoti -"

Micchā (adv.): untruth, wrongly, in a wrong way, wrong / false
Hoti (bhavati): to be, exists (in this context: "one who is")

micchaditthiko:
ditthi: views
दृष्टि (drishti): view, vision

micchasankappo:
sankappa: aspiration (thought)
आकांक्षा (akanksha): aspiration

micchavaco:
vaca: speech
वाक् (vak)

micchakammanto:
kammanta (kamma + anta): final/ending action
कर्मानता (karmanta): duty

miccha-ajivo:
ajiva: livelihood
आजीविका (ajivika)

micchavayamo:
vayama: effort
प्रयास (?)

micchasati:
sati: awareness, mindfulness

micchasamadhi:
samadhi: concentration++
समाधि (samadhi)

micchāñāṇī:
āñāṇī: ...knowledge? somehow?
