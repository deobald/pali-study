0. Lekhasuttaṃ

Lekha: writing, inscription, or drawing

--

1. "Tayome, bhikkhave, puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmiṃ. Katame tayo?"

tayome => tayo: three (3) people
-ime => ayam: these (plural)

puggalā => puggalo: individual, person (plural)

santo => atthi: to exist

saṃvijjamānā => Saŋvijjati: existing (aggitated, moved, stirred)

lokasmiṃ => loka: world (existing within)

Katame tayo? = "which three?"

--

2. "pāsāṇalekhūpamo puggalo, pathavilekhūpamo puggalo, udakalekhūpamo puggalo."

puggalo: individual (sing.)

ūpamo => upama: "coming quite or nearly up to", like, similar, equal
lekha: writing, inscription, letter, epistle
> infer "inscribed" or "a line" from the definition + context of 2500 years ago
> the Pali word for "line" is "pali" :)

pāsāṇalekhūpamo => pāsāṇalekh: rock inscription [pāsāṇa = rock]
pathavilekhūpamo => paṭhavi: earth / frequent in representing the earth as solid, firm, spacious ground
udakalekhūpamo => udaka: water

--

3. "Katamo ca, bhikkhave, pāsāṇalekhūpamo puggalo?"

pāsāṇalekh: rock inscriptions

Which indeed (ca) are individuals (puggalo) like rock inscriptions, monks?

--

4. "Idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo abhiṇhaṃ kujjhati. So ca khvassa kodho
dīgharattaṃ anuseti."

Idha: here, in this world

ekacco => ekacca: 1. some, a few, "one like" [eka = one]
                  2. "a certain one"

abhiṇhaṃ [contracted form of abhikkhaṇaŋ]: repeatedly, often, continuous
अभिना

kujjhati: to be angry

so: he
ca: now
khvassa => kho: indeed / surely
kodho => kodha: anger
> kodha is one of the obstacles to Arahantship,
  and freedom from kodha is one of the fundamental virtues of a well-balanced mind.

dīgharattaṃ: 1. a long time (adv.)
             2. a long night (arratri: night)
digha: long

anuseti: literally: "lies dormant" / "to lie down with"
         meaning: to dwell on [anger]
anu: follows
seti: lying
anukilesha: sleeping volcanoes

--

5. "seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, pāsāṇe lekhā na khippaṃ lujjati vātena vā udakena vā,
ciraṭṭhitikā hoti;"

seyyathāpi: just as (introduce a simile)
pāsāṇe => pāsāṇa: stone, rock (locative: "on a rock")
lekha: writing

na: not
khippaṃ: quick (literally "like a shot" / throwing)
lujjati: breaks up, falls apart
> "lujjati ti loka" - that which is being constantly destroyed is loka

> Question: How do we know vata and udaka in this sentence act on pasane lekha?

vātena => vāta: wind, air
vā: or
udakena => udaka: water

ciraṭṭhitikā: long-lasting
cira: long time
titthati: lasting

--

6. "evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, idhekacco puggalo abhiṇhaṃ kujjhati."

evamevaṃ: "thus, thus" literally (translated as "similarly, ")
kho: indeed (emphatic)

idhekacco => idha + ekacco: here, in this place + some, a few
puggalo: individuals
abhiṇhaṃ: repeatedly
kujjhati: are angry

--

7. "so ca khvassa kodho dīgharattaṃ anuseti. ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, pāsāṇalekhūpamo puggalo.

so: he
ca: now
khvassa => kho: surely (emphatic)
kodho: anger
dīgharattaṃ: for a long time
anuseti: to dwell on (lit. "to lie down with")

ayaṃ: this person
vuccati: to be called
pāsāṇa + lekh + ūpamo + puggalo: rock + inscription + like + person

--

8. "katamo ca, bhikkhave, pathavilekhūpamo puggalo?"

katama: which
ca: now
paṭhavi (earth) + lekh (writing) + upamo (similar)
puggalo: individuals

--

9. "idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo abhiṇhaṃ kujjhati.
    so ca khvassa kodho na dīgharattaṃ anuseti."

idha: here, in this world
ekacco: some, a few (eka = one)
puggalo: individuals
abhiṇhaṃ: repeatedly
kujjhati: to be angry with

so: he, the individual
ca: now
khvassa (kho): indeed, surely (emphatic)
kodho: anger (synonym: āghāta)
na: not
dīgharattaṃ: for a long time
anuseti: 1. "to lie down with"
         2. (of the idea) to obsess, to fill the mind persistently, to lie dormant &
            be continually cropping up.

--

10. "seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, pathaviyā lekhā khippaṃ lujjati vātena vā udakena vā, na
     ciraṭṭhitikā hoti;"

seyyathāpi: just as
pathaviya => pathavi: earth
lekha: writing
khippaṃ: 1. quick, lit. in the way of throwing (cp. "like a shot")
lujjati: break up, fall apart

Question: how does wind/water act upon earth-writing?

vātena => vata => vayo: wind
va: or
udakena => udaka: water (˚assa dhārā gushes or showers of w.)
na: not
ciraṭṭhitikā: long-lasting, perpetual

--

11. "evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, idhekacco puggalo abhiṇhaṃ kujjhati."

evamevaṃ => evam: thus (similarly)
telugu: "illa" => "illailla"
kho: emphatic
idhekacco => idha + ekacca: here, in this world, there is one
puggalo: individuals
abhiṇhaṃ: repeatedly, continuously
kujjhati: become angry

--

12. "so ca khvassa kodho na dīgharattaṃ anuseti."

so: this (anger)
ca: but / and (connecting to previous sentence)
khvassa => kho: indeed
kodho: anger
na: not
dīgharattaṃ: for a long time
anuseti: 1. "to lie down with"
         2. (of the idea) to obsess, to fill the mind persistently, to lie dormant &
            be continually cropping up.

--

13. "ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, pathavilekhūpamo puggalo."

ayaṃ: this person
vuccati: so called
pathavi (earth) + lekh (writing) + ūpamo (similar to)
puggalo: individuals

--

14. "katamo ca, bhikkhave, udakalekhūpamo puggalo?"

katamo => katama: which?
ca: and
udaka (water) + lekh (writing) + ūpamo (similar to)
puggalo: individuals

--

15. "idha, bhikkhave, ekacco puggalo āgāḷhenapi vuccamāno pharusenapi vuccamāno
     amanāpenapi vuccamāno sandhiyatimeva saṃsandatimeva sammodatimeva."

idha: here, in this world
ekacco: some, a few
puggalo: individuals
āgāḷhenapi => āgāḷha: strong, hard, harsh, rough (in speech)
           => api: even (even when)
galha: "throat" (when someone is speaking loudly, "galhagalha")

vuccamāno => vuccamāna: saying or calling (addressed)
pharusenapi => pharu: rough, harsh, unkind, cruel
> Sanskrit: परशा / parusha, not to be confused with पुरुष / purusha
vuccamāno: saying or calling
amanāpenapi => amanāpa (detestful) + pi
            => manapa (agreeable) vs. amanāpa (disagreeable)
pi: also, even, just so, although, even if
vuccamāno: saying or calling
sandhiyatimeva => sandhi (union/break) + yat (?) + im (?) + eva (only):
sandhi: 1. union, junction, joint
        2. breach, break, hole, chasm
        3. sandhiyati => epithetical use of the monk named "Sandha"?
saṃsandatimeva => saṃsandati + me + va
saṃsandati: 1. to run together, to associate with
             2. put together, unite, combine
sammodatimeva => sammodati + me + va
sammodati: 1. to rejoice, delight
           2. to agree with, exchange friendly greeting with

--

16. "seyyathāpi, bhikkhave, udake lekhā khippaṃyeva paṭivigacchati, na ciraṭṭhitikā hoti;"

seyyathāpi: just as
udake: water
lekhā: writing
khippaṃ (quickly) + yeva (even, just, also)
paṭivigacchati: go apart (again), go away, go asunder
na ciraṭṭhitikā hoti: does not last long

--

17. "evamevaṃ kho, bhikkhave, idhekacco puggalo āgāḷhenapi vuccamāno pharusenapi
     vuccamāno amanāpenapi vuccamāno sandhiyatimeva saṃsandatimeva sammodatimeva."

evamevaṃ: only
kho: indeed
idhekacco => idha + ekacco: here in there world there are some
puggalo: individuals

āgāḷhenapi => āgāḷha: strong, hard, harsh, rough (in speech)
vuccamāno => vuccamāna: saying or calling
pharusenapi => pharu: rough, harsh, unkind, cruel
> Sanskrit: परशा / parusha, not to be confused with पुरुष / purusha
vuccamāno: saying or calling
amanāpenapi => amanāpa (detestful) + pi
pi: also, even, just so, although, even if
vuccamāno: saying or calling

sandhi (euphonic agreement, pleasing to the ear) + yati + me + va
saṃsandati (fits, agrees, runs together) + me + va
sammodati (rejoices, delights, exchanges friendly greetings) + me + va

--

18. "ayaṃ vuccati, bhikkhave, udakalekhūpamo puggalo."

ayaṃ: this person
vuccati: is said
udaka (water) + lekh (writing) + upamo (similar to)
puggalo: individuals

--

19. "ime kho, bhikkhave, tayo puggalā santo saṃvijjamānā lokasmin."

ime => ayaṃ: this person
kho: indeed
tayo (ti: three) + puggalā (persons) + santo (atthi: to be, to exist)
saṃvijjamānā: existing (to be stirred, aggitated, moved)
