0. Dhammacakkappavattanasuttaṃ

Dhamma: Nature
Cakka: wheel
pavattana: carrying out, moving forward; doing good, beneficial, useful

--

1. "Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā bārāṇasiyaṃ viharati
   isipatane migadāye."

Ekaṃ: one
samayaṃ => samay: time
bhagavā: blessed one, exhalted one, "Lord" (commonly misunderstood to mean "god")
bārāṇasiyaṃ: in Varanasi
viharati: residing
Isipatane: residing place of the Pañcavaggiya monks:
    Kondañña (Aññā Kondañña),
    Bhaddiya,
    Vappa,
    Mahānāma
    Assaji
migadāye: miga (deer) + dāye (gift / park)
- supposedly won from a king by a deer willing to give his own life to save a pregnant doe

--

2. "Tatra kho bhagavā pañcavaggiye bhikkhū
    āmantesi."

Tatra: "here and there" but in this particular case it means "in that place"
kho: emphatic
bhagavā: blessed one
pañcavaggiye bhikkhū: the five monks
āmantesi: call, address, invite, consult

--

3. "dveme, bhikkhave, antā pabbajitena na
    sevitabbā. Katame dve?

dveme: dwe (two) + me (these)
antā: ends, goals
pabbajitena => pabbajati: monk; one who has gone from the home
na sevitabbā => sevati: (not) to serve, to associate with
Katame dve: which two?
