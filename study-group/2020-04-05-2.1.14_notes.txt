110.
yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, dussīlo asamāhito.
ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, sīlavantassa jhāyino. 

yo - anyone (pron)
ca - then
vassasataṃ = vassa (the year) + sata (hundred)
jīve - living, life

dussīlo = du + sila
asamāhito = a + samāhito 
 samāhito = sam (much) + hita (benefit, useful)
 or pp. of Samādahati = concentrated

 ekāha - one day
 jīvitaṃ - to live
 seyyo - (nom. fem.) seyya = better, excellent

 sīlavantassa - sila + vantassa (gives up?)
 jhāyino - (nom.) jhāyati & jhāna = meditates

111.
yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, duppañño asamāhito.
ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, paññavantassa jhāyino. 

yo ca vassasataṃ jīve - when anyone lives 100 years
duppañño = du + pañña = foolish
asamāhito = a + samāhito = unconcentrated, uncontrolled
ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo - better to live one day
paññavantassa jhāyino - pañña + vantassa = develop wisdom? - jhāyino = meditates

115.
yo ca vassasataṃ jīve, apassaṃ dhammamuttamaṃ.
ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo, passato dhammamuttamaṃ. 

yo ca vassasataṃ jīve - when anyone lives 100 years
apassaṃ - a + passati = not seeing/finding/understanding
ekāhaṃ jīvitaṃ seyyo - better to live one day
passato - (nom.) seeing/finding/understanding
