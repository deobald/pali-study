
sammādiṭṭhisuttaṃ (MN 9)

> Evaṃ me sutaṃ:
- Thus it was heard by me 

> Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā sāvatthiyaṃ viharati
> jetavane anāthapiṇḍikassa ārāme.
- One time the bhagavā was staying in Savatthi, 
  in Anathapindaka's pleasure park - Jetavana 
  (the park purchased by Anathapindaka from Prince Jetakumara)

> Tatra kho āyasmā sāriputto bhikkhū āmantesi–
  “āvuso bhikkhave”ti.
- Then the venerable Sariputta addressed the bhikkus:
  "friends/brothers, bhikkhus" he said

āyasmā (ayasmant) - venerable
avuso - friend/brother - address to monks

> “Avuso”ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosuṃ.
- "Friend" the bhikkus replied to venerable Sariputta

paccassosuṃ (paṭissuṇāti) - agreement

> Ayasmā Sāriputto etadavoca:
- venerable Sariputta said this:

> Sammādiṭṭhi 'sammādiṭṭhī'ti, āvuso, vuccati.
- friends, one with right view is called "one with right view"

> Kittāvatā nu kho, āvuso, ariyasāvako sammādiṭṭhi hoti,
- in what respect, friends, is a noble disciple with right view

kittāvatā = ki + t + tāvatā - how far? in what respect?
tāvatā - on that account, by that much

> ujugatāssa diṭṭhi, dhamme aveccappasādena samannāgato,

ujugata - straight, upright
aveccappasādena = avecca + p + pasādena 
  avecca - certainly, definitely, perfectly
  pasādena - joy, faith, clarity
samannāgato (samannāgata) - endowed with, possessed of

> āgato imaṃ saddhamman”ti?

āgato (āgata) - arrived at 
imaṃ - this person 
saddhamman - the true doctrine


> “Dūratopi kho mayaṃ, āvuso, āgaccheyyāma

Dūratopi (dura) - far
mayaṃ - we
āgaccheyyāma (āgacchati 1st. pl. opt./pot.) - [could] have come
  - potential case?

> āyasmato sāriputtassa santike etassa bhāsitassa atthamaññātuṃ.
- venerable Sariputta

santike - near
etassa - this
bhāsitassa - to speak of ^
atthamaññātuṃ = atth + am + aññ + ātuṃ ?
  atthi - benefit
  añña (jnana) - knowledge, insight

> Sādhu vatāyasmantaṃyeva sāriputtaṃ paṭibhātu etassa bhāsitassa attho.

sādhu - good, virtuous, pious
vatāyasmantaṃyeva = vat + āyasmant + aṃ + y + eva	
  vata - excl. "surely", "indeed"
  āyasmant - venerable
  eva - emph. "only"
paṭibhātu (paṭibhāti) - make evident
etassa - this
bhāsitassa (dat. - "to us") - speak about
atthi - existential

> Ayasmato sāriputtassa sutvā bhikkhū dhāressantī”ti.
Having heard this from the venerable Sariputta, the Bhikkhus will remember.

sāriputtassa (gen.) - "from Sariputta"
sutvā - heard
dhāressantī (dhāreti, 3rd. pl. fut.) - know by heart, understand


> “Tena hi, āvuso, suṇātha, sādhukaṃ manasi karotha, bhāsissāmī”ti.
- Then friends, listen, closely and attentively, to whate I have to say

suṇātha (suṇāti 2nd. pl.) - "you listen"
sādhukaṃ - well, thoroughly
manasi - mind
karotha - do, act 

> “Evamāvuso”ti kho te bhikkhū āyasmato sāriputtassa paccassosuṃ. 
- "Just so" the Bhikkhus replied to Sariputta

> yasmā sāriputto etadavoca:
Then Sariputta said this:



> “Yato kho, āvuso, ariyasāvako akusalañca pajānāti, akusalamūlañca pajānāti,
- When, friends, the noble disciple understands what is wholesome and what is unwholesome

yato - whence since, when 
akusalañca (akusala + ca) - unwholesome 
pajānāti - understands 
akusalamūlañca = akusala + mūla (root) + ca (what)

> kusalañca pajānāti, kusalamūlañca pajānāti,
- ... understands what is wholesome and what is the root of the wholesome ...

> ettāvatāpi kho, āvuso, ariyasāvako sammādiṭṭhi hoti,
- it is in that very way indeed, friends, that the noble disciple is one with right view

ettāvatāpi (ettāvatā) - to that extent

> Ujugatāssa diṭṭhi, dhamme aveccappasādena samannāgato, āgato imaṃ saddhammaṃ.

ujugata - straight, upright
aveccappasādena = avecca + p + pasādena 
  avecca - certainly, definitely, perfectly
  pasādena - joy, faith, clarity
samannāgato (samannāgata) - endowed with, possessed of

āgato (āgata) - arrived at 
imaṃ - this person 
saddhamman - the true doctrine

> Katamaṃ panāvuso, akusalaṃ, katamaṃ akusalamūlaṃ,
- what is the unwholesome what is the root of the unwholesome?

panāvuso - pana (interrogative) + avuso ("friend")

> Katamaṃ kusalaṃ, katamaṃ kusalamūlaṃ?
- what is wholesome what is the root of the wholesome?

> Pāṇātipāto kho, āvuso, akusalaṃ, adinnādānaṃ akusalaṃ,

Pāṇātipāto = Pāṇā + atipāta
  Pāṇā - life, breath
  atipāto - killing

adinnādānaṃ = adinna + dānaṃ
  adinna - that which is not given
  dānaṃ - taking gifts

> kāmesumicchācāro akusalaṃ, musāvādo akusalaṃ,

kāmesumicchācāro
  kāmesu (kāma) - pleasure, lust
  micchācāro - wrong behaviour

musāvādo
  musā - falsehood, lie
  vādo (vac) - saying, speech

> pisuṇā vācā akusalaṃ, pharusā vācā akusalaṃ, samphappalāpo akusalaṃ,

pisuṇā - slander, malicious speech 
pharusā - rough, harsh, unkind
samphappalāpo = sampha + p + palāpo
  sampha - frivolity, useless speech
  palāpo - nonsense

> abhijjhā akusalaṃ, byāpādo akusalaṃ, micchādiṭṭhi akusalaṃ.
- covetousness/craving, aversion/ill-will/malevolence, wrong view ...are unwholesome.

> idaṃ vuccatāvuso akusalaṃ.
- this is called unwholesome.


> Katamañcāvuso, akusalamūlaṃ?
- what is the root of the unwholesome?

> Lobho akusalamūlaṃ, doso akusalamūlaṃ, moho akusalamūlaṃ.
- greed, dosa = anger/corruption, moha = ignorance/stupidity/delusion ...are the root of the unwholesome.

> idaṃ vuccatāvuso, akusalamūlaṃ.
- this is called the root of the unwholesome.


> Katamañcāvuso, kusalaṃ?
- what is the root of the wholesome?

> Pāṇātipātā veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstaining from killing living beings

veramaṇī - abstinence

> adinnādānā veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstain from stealing

> kāmesumicchācārā veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstain from sexual misconduct

> musāvādā veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstain from speaking lies 

> pisuṇāya vācāya veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstain from slander

> pharusāya vācāya veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstain from rough speech

> samphappalāpā veramaṇī kusalaṃ,
- abstain from frivolous/useless speech & nonsense

> anabhijjhā kusalaṃ,
- being free of craving/covetousness

> abyāpādo kusalaṃ,
- being free of malevolence/ill-will/aversion

> sammādiṭṭhi kusalaṃ–
- holding right view 

> idaṃ vuccatāvuso, kusalaṃ.
- this is called wholesome, friends.



> Katamañcāvuso, kusalamūlaṃ?
- what is the root of the wholesome, friends?

> Alobho kusalamūlaṃ,
- non-greed

> adoso kusalamūlaṃ,
- non-malevolence

> amoho kusalamūlaṃ.
- non-ignorance

> idaṃ vuccatāvuso, kusalamūlaṃ.
- this is called the root of the wholesome, friends.



> Yato kho, āvuso, ariyasāvako evaṃ akusalaṃ pajānāti, 
- when, friends, the noble disciple understands what is unwholesome

> evaṃ akusalamūlaṃ pajānāti, 
- he understands the root of the unwholesome

> evaṃ kusalaṃ pajānāti, evaṃ kusalamūlaṃ pajānāti,
- he understands the wholesome; he understands the root of the wholesome

> so sabbaso rāgānusayaṃ pahāya,
- he gives up all underlying tendencies toward craving

> paṭighānusayaṃ paṭivinodetvā,
- ... has dispelled underlying tendencies toward anger ...

paṭigha - anger

> ‘asmī’ti diṭṭhimānānusayaṃ samūhanitvā,
- ... has completely uprooted the underlying tendency toward the view "I am"

samūhanitvā = sam (completely) + ūhanitvā (uproot)

> avijjaṃ pahāya vijjaṃ uppādetvā, diṭṭhevadhamme dukkhassantakaro hoti.
- having left ignorance and given rise to wisdom, he sees only the true dhamma
  and puts and end to suffering

diṭṭhevadhamme = diṭṭhi (see) + eva (only, emph.) + dhamme (true dhamma)
dukkhassantakaro = dukkha + ss + antakaro (put an end to)



> Ettāvatāpi kho, āvuso, ariyasāvako sammādiṭṭhi hoti, 
- Indeed to that (as follows) extent, friends, a noble disciple has right view

ettāvatāpi (ettāvatā) - to that extent

> ujugatāssa diṭṭhi, dhamme aveccappasādena samannāgato, 

ujugata - straight, upright
aveccappasādena = avecca + p + pasādena 
  avecca - certainly, definitely, perfectly
  pasādena - joy, faith, clarity
samannāgato (samannāgata) - endowed with, possessed of

> āgato imaṃ saddhamman”ti.

āgato (āgata) - arrived at 
imaṃ - this person 
saddhamman - the true doctrine
