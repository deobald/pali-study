207.

> So kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, uppannuppannaṃ

so ahaṃ - "this I"
uppannuppannaṃ - uppanna (pp. uppajati: arisen) + uppannaṃ = "whenever it has arisen"
    - the doubling causes uppajati to mean "whenever"

> kāmavitakkaṃ pajahameva vinodameva

kāmavitakkaṃ - kāma (lust) + vitakkaṃ (thoughts)
pajahameva (n.) - to abandon      => "I have done abandonment (n.) to it"
vinodameva (n.) - to remove       => "I have done removal (n.) to it"
byantameva (n.) - to make an end  => "I have done an ending (n.) to it"

> byantameva naṃ akāsiṃ.

byantameva - an ending
akāsiṃ - "I have done" (past. of "karoti")


208.

> Tassa mayhaṃ, bhikkhave, evaṃ appamattassa

tassa - this
mayhaṃ - ahaṃ (for/of/while this I)
evaṃ - thus
appamattassa - vigilant, heedful

> ātāpino pahitattassa viharato uppajjati

- all in genative case

ātāpino - ātāpi
pahitattassa - pahita [pp. of padahati] (resolute, energetic)
viharato - pp. residing = resided / dwelled
uppajjati - there arises

> byāpādavitakko ...pe... uppajjati vihiṃsāvitakko.

byāpādavitakko - byāpāda (ill-will, malevolence) + vitakko (reflection / thought)
vihiṃsāvitakko - vihiṃsā (cruelty, injuring) + vitakko (thought)

vihiṃsā vs. ahiṃsā

> So (it) evaṃ (thus) pajānāmi (I understood clearly):

> ‘uppanno kho me ayaṃ vihiṃsāvitakko. So ca

it arises in (for) me, this thought of vihiṃsā (cruelty). and it

> kho attabyābādhāyapi saṃvattati,

atta (self) + byābādhāyapi (harms)
by (vi => bya as sandi) + ābādhā (disease, affliction)
byābādhā + aya (toward / dat.) + pi
saṃ+vattati - leads to

> Parabyābādhāyapi saṃvattati,

para (others) + byābādhāyapi (harms)

> ubhayabyābādhāyapi saṃvattati,

ubhaya (both) + byābādhāyapi (harms)

> paññānirodhiko vighātapakkhiko anibbānasaṃvattaniko’

paññānirodhiko - cessation of wisdom
vighātapakkhiko - siding with destruction
    vighāta: destruction; distress; vexation; annoyance. (m.)
    pakkhika: belonging to a faction; siding with; fortnightly. (adj.)
anibbānasaṃvattaniko’ - is NOT conducive to nibbana
    nibbāna: cooling; extinction (of a fire);
             emancipation; the final bliss. (nt.)
    saṃvattanika: conducive to; involving. (adj.)
    saṃvattatī: leads to

> ‘Attabyābādhāya saṃvattatī’tipi me, bhikkhave,

Attabyābādhāya - harming myself / self-affliction
saṃvattatī - leads to

> paṭisañcikkhato abbhatthaṃ gacchati;

paṭisañcikkhato - pp. "when I reflected" / "while reflecting"
    paṭi - opposite
    saṃ - emphasis
    cikkhati - to declare
    => to declare, but inside = "considering"
abbhatthaṃ - abhi (toward) + attha (home)
gacchati - to go
abbhatthaṃ gacchati - idiom meaning "disappearing" / "going away" literally "to go toward home"

> ‘parabyābādhāya saṃvattatī’tipi me, bhikkhave,

para - others

> paṭisañcikkhato abbhatthaṃ gacchati;

> ‘ubhayabyābādhāya saṃvattatī’tipi me,

ubhaya - both

> bhikkhave, paṭisañcikkhato abbhatthaṃ gacchati;

> ‘paññānirodhiko vighātapakkhiko

paññānirodhiko - cessation of panna
vighātapakkhiko - vighāta (destruction) + pakkhiko (siding with)

> anibbānasaṃvattaniko’tipi me,

a + nibbāna + saṃvattaniko - leading away from nibbana

> bhikkhave, paṭisañcikkhato abbhatthaṃ gacchati.

paṭisañcikkhato - "when reflecting"
abbhatthaṃ gacchati - "going toward home" = "disappear"

> So kho ahaṃ, bhikkhave, uppannuppannaṃ

whenever [this] arises for me

> vihiṃsāvitakkaṃ... pajahameva vinodameva

vihiṃsā (violence) + vitakkaṃ (thought)
pajahameva - abandonment
vinodameva - removal

> byantameva naṃ akāsiṃ.

byantameva (anta) - ending
akāsiṃ - "I have done" (past. of "karoti")

> Yaññadeva, bhikkhave, bhikkhu

yaṃ - which, whichever
yaññadeva - whichever [bhikku]

> bahulamanuvitakketi anuvicāreti,

bahulamanuvitakketi = bahulam (abundant, frequently) + anu (repetition) + vitakketi (thoughts)
anuvicāreti - anu (repeatedly) + vicara (rolling in thoughts, mulled over)

> tathā tathā nati hoti cetaso.

tathā tathā - "thus thus"
nati - inclination
hoti - is
cetaso (gen.) - "of the mind"

> Kāmavitakkaṃ ce, bhikkhave, bhikkhu bahulamanuvitakketi anuvicāreti,

kāma (lust) + vitakkaṃ (thoughts)
bahula (much, frequent) + m + anuvitakketi (reflects, ponders)
    anu (after, according to) + vi (emph.) + takk (logic / argument) + eti ("he thinks" = active, present, 3rd, sing.)
    takk = तर्क (hi/sk)
anu (after, according to) + vi (emph.) + cār (careti: one who walks, frequents) + eti

> pahāsi nekkhammavitakkaṃ,

pahāsi = seems wrong: makes one laugh; gladdens. (pa + has + e ... seems wrong)
       = Pahāsi is 3rd sg. aor. of paharati;
         -> paharati: to strike, hit, beat / join to
       = harati
         -> [bring?], take, steal, remove
       = 3rd sg. aor of pajahati
         -> pajahati: to give up, renounce, forsake, abandon, let go

...pajahati + asi (2nd. sing.?)

nekkhamma (renunciation, dispassion) + vitakkaṃ (thoughts of)

> kāmavitakkaṃ bahulamakāsi,

kāma (lust) + vitakkaṃ (thoughts)
bahula (much, frequent) + m + akāsi (did, made, or performed. aor. of karoti.)

> tassa taṃ kāmavitakkāya cittaṃ namati.

tassa: sing. dat./gen. = "for/of him"
tam: accusative "that thing"
kāma (lust) + vitakkāya (thought, dat. "thought for/to ..him? or the kāmavitakkaṃ?")
cittaṃ (nom. / acc. -- probably accusative = "his mind")
namati (nameti) - "it bows down"
