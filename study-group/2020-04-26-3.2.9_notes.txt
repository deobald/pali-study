
anattalakkhanasuttam (SN 22.59)

anatta + lakkhana + sutta.m

anatta - non-self
lakkhana - characteristic
         - of what? anicca, dukkha, anatta

> Ekaṃ samayaṃ bhagavā bārāṇasiyaṃ viharati isipatane migadāye.
one time the buddha was staying in varanasi -> isipatana -> deer park

viharati - (hist. pres. tense)
miga - beast, deer
daya - woods

> Tatra kho bhagavā pañcavaggiye bhikkhū āmantesi:
- there the bhagava addressed the group-of-five bhikkhūs

pañcavaggiye (acc.)
vaggiya - group of, party
=> vagga - group
amanteti - to call, to speak

> ‘‘Bhikkhavo’’ti. ‘‘Bhadante’’ti te bhikkhū bhagavato paccassosuṃ. Bhagavā etadavoca :
- 'bhikkhus.' 'Bhante' they replied. The Buddha said this:

bhadante = bhadra (safety) + anta (end) = "one who has the final safety"
=> shortened to bhante
bhagavato (dat.) - "to the bhagava"
paccassosuṃ (paṭissuṇāti) - to assert agreement
etadavoca (etad + avoca) - said this


> ‘‘Rūpaṃ, bhikkhave, anattā. 
- rupa is not self, bhukkhus
- hoti implied

> Rūpañca hidaṃ, bhikkhave, attā abhavissa, 
- and if this thing (rupa) were/would-have-been self...

hidaṃ (nom.) - this thing (rupa)
attā (nom.) - self (object)
abhavissa (bhavi, active cond.) - "if it would have been"
  => always comes when something is not possible ("if pigs could fly" etc.)

> nayidaṃ rūpaṃ ābādhāya saṃvatteyya, labbhetha ca rūpe:
- this thing, rupa, would not lead to disease, and [you] would/could obtain the body thus:

ābādhāya (ābādhā) - disease, affliction
saṃvatteyya (saṃvattati, 3rd opt./pot.) - would lead to
labbhetha (labbhati, labh + ya, 2nd pl. opt.) - to be obtained
rupe (loc.) - in time?


> ‘Evaṃ me rūpaṃ hotu, evaṃ me rūpaṃ mā ahosī’ti.
- let rupa be [thus] for me, in this way let rupa not be [thus] for me.

evaṃ - thus
rūpaṃ (nom./acc.) - object of sentence
me (dat./gen.) - of/for/to me/mine
hotu (fut.) - to be
ahosī (literally past) - to be
  => 'mā' verbs are always past-tense but don't necessarily mean past

> Yasmā ca kho, bhikkhave, rūpaṃ anattā, 
- but as the body is not self,

yasmā / tasmā

> tasmā rūpaṃ ābādhāya saṃvattati, na ca labbhati rūpe:
- thus the body leads to disease, and one cannot obtain [with regard to] form thus:

ābādhāya (ābādhā) - disease, affliction
saṃvattati (saṃvattati, 3rd opt./pot.) - would lead to
labbhati (pres. pass.) - to be obtained
rupe (loc.) - in time?

> ‘Evaṃ me rūpaṃ hotu, evaṃ me rūpaṃ mā ahosī’’’ti.

> ‘‘Taṃ kiṃ maññatha, bhikkhave, rūpaṃ niccaṃ vā aniccaṃ vā’’ti?
- what do you imagine, bhikkhus, that rupa is permanent or impermanent?

taṃ - that 
kiṃ - what
maññatha (maññati 2nd. pl.) - imagine / think

> ‘‘Aniccaṃ, bhante.’’ti

> ‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vā taṃ sukhaṃ vā’’ti?
- that which is impermanent is suffering or pleasure [happiness]?

yaṃ / taṃ - contrasting pronouns = whatever / this
panāniccaṃ - pana + anicca (interrogative)

> ‘‘Dukkhaṃ, bhante.’’ti

> ‘‘Yaṃ panāniccaṃ dukkhaṃ vipariṇāmadhammaṃ, 
- regarding that which is impermanent, suffering, and subject to change,

vipariṇāmadhammaṃ = vipariṇāma (change, for the worse) + dhammaṃ (subject to)

> kallaṃ nu taṃ samanupassituṃ :
- is it proper to see it like this :

kallaṃ (kalla) - proper
nu - interrogative particle (with pronoun)
taṃ (ta) - that thing
samanupassituṃ (saṃ + anu + passati, tuṃ = infinitive) - to see, to perceive
