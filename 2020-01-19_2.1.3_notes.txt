0. Abhiṇhapaccavekkhitabbaṭhānasuttaṃ

Abhiṇha: continuous; habitual
paccavekkhitabba (paccavekkhati): consider; review; contemplate
ṭhāna: condition; reason; cause

"born of one's own kamma"?

--

1. "Pañcimāni, bhikkhave, ṭhānāni abhiṇhaṃ
paccavekkhitabbāni itthiyā vā purisena vā
gahaṭṭhena vā pabbajitena vā."

Pañcimāni (panca): five
-imāni: these
ṭhānāni (ṭhā / ṭhitati: to stand):
स्तिथि - posture
स्तन - country / place
- place / locality
- fact; theme
- condition; reason; cause
abhiṇhaṃ: often; repeatedly
paccavekkhitabbāni: (should) consider; review; contemplate
- pati: backwards; against; opposite
- avekkhati: to see / to look (outside)
- pati + avekkhati: opposite of "look outside" = "look inside"
- tabbāni: should (passive future participle)
vā: or (comes after all the possibilities; see Satipatthana Sutta)
itthiyā (itthi): woman
-iyā: "by" (feminine instrumental case)
purisena (purisa): man
gahaṭṭhena (gahaṭṭha): householder
gaha-: house
-ṭṭha-: one who lives in (stands) ^ in a house
pabbajitena (pabbajita): monk; reluse
-ena: "by" (masculine instrumental case)

Endings of nouns / genders:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=joWLbqYxX8o

2. "Katamāni pañca?"

Katamāni: which?
pañca: five

--

3. "‘Jarādhammomhi, jaraṃ anatīto’ti
abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhitabbaṃ itthiyā vā purisena
vā gahaṭṭhena vā pabbajitena vā."

Jarādhammomhi: subject to decay
- dhamma / dhammomhi: nature of
- -omhi: first person ("I am")
jaraṃ (jara): old; decayed; decrepit
anatīto: future (atīta/atīto = past; gone by)
- also, literally "I cannot go past this"

abhiṇhaṃ (often) paccavekkhitabbaṃ (contemplate) itthiyā (woman) vā purisena (man) vā
gahaṭṭhena (householder) vā pabbajitena (monk) vā.

--

4. "‘Byādhidhammomhi, byādhiṃ anatīto’ti
abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhitabbaṃ itthiyā vā purisena
vā gahaṭṭhena vā pabbajitena vā."

Byādhidhammomhi (byādhi): by nature subject to sickness/disease
byādhiṃ: sickness, disease
anatīto: future (atīto = past; gone by) => cannot escape / go past

abhiṇhaṃ etc. -- see 3.

5. "‘Maraṇadhammomhi, maraṇaṃ anatīto’ti
abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhitabbaṃ itthiyā vā purisena
vā gahaṭṭhena vā pabbajitena vā."

Maraṇadhammomhi (maran): by nature subject death
maraṇaṃ: death
anatīto: future

abhiṇhaṃ etc. -- see 3.

6. "‘Sabbehi me piyehi manāpehi nānābhāvo vinābhāvo’ti
abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhitabbaṃ itthiyā vā purisena
vā gahaṭṭhena vā pabbajitena vā."

sabbehi: all; everything
- "-ehi": plural
me: to me; mine
piyehi (piya): dear; beloved
manāpehi (manapa): pleasing, charming
- often "piya manapa"
- manapa: agreeable
nānābhāvo (nānābhāva): separation
- "-bhavo" is nominative (present tense)
- nānā: different
vinābhāvo (vinābhāva): separation
वीना (hindi): without

- interestingly, future tense verb needs to be assumed (no existential verb is in the sentence)

7. "‘Kammassakomhi, kammadāyādo kammayoni
kammabandhu kammapaṭisaraṇo,
yaṃ kammaṃ karissāmi – kalyāṇaṃ vā pāpakaṃ
vā– tassa dāyādo bhavissāmī’ti
abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhitabbaṃ itthiyā vā purisena
vā gahaṭṭhena vā pabbajitena vā."

Kammassakomhi => komhi: possession?
"-saka": one's own (possession)
"-mhi": first person
kammadāyādo => dāyāda: inheritance
kammayoni => yoni: womb (born of kamma)
kammabandhu => bandhu: kinsman; relative; relation
kammapaṭisaraṇo => paṭisaraṇa: refuge; shelter; protection
"-pati": either "opposite of" or (in this case) intensifies
sarana: refuge

yaṃ: which; whatever thing
kammaṃ: kamma
karissāmi (karo): do; perform (future tense)
kalyāṇaṃ (kalyaan): beautiful, auspicious, morally helpful
va: or
pāpakaṃ (pāpaka): wicked; wretched; sinful
tassa (ta): that; that thing
dāyādo / dāyāda: inheritance
bhavissāmī: is; (my) state of existence; future tense = "heir"

abhiṇhaṃ, etc. - see 3.

8. "“Kiñca, bhikkhave, atthavasaṃ paṭicca,

‘kammassakomhi, kammadāyādo kammayoni

vacīduccaritaṃ manoduccaritaṃ."

Kiñca (kim): what?; who?; which?
atthavasaṃ (atthavasa): reason; use
paṭicca: because of; concerning

kammassakomhi, etc - see No. 7

abhiṇhaṃ, etc - see No. 3

9. "Tassa taṃ ṭhānaṃ abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhato sabbaso
vā duccaritaṃ pahīyati tanu vā pana hoti."

Tassa: this; that thing
taṃ: that
ṭhānaṃ: condition; reason; cause
abhiṇhaṃ: often; repeatedly
paccavekkhato: contemplate
sabbaso: all; every
duccarita: bad conduct; wrong action
pahīyati: passes away; is abandoned
tanu: thin; slender
pana: and yet; but; and now; moreover
- vā pana: specifically "or else"
hoti: becomes

pahīyati tanu vā pana hoti
=> "is abandoned [or else] becomes very thin"

--

‘kammassakomhi, kammadāyādo kammayoni

yaṃ kammaṃ karissāmi– kalyāṇaṃ vā pāpakaṃ

abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhitabbaṃ itthiyā vā purisena

Idaṃ: this thing
kho: indeed (emphatic)
atthavasaṃ: reason; use
paṭicca: on account of; concerning

kammassakomhi, etc - see No. 7

11. "...“Sa kho so, bhikkhave, ariyasāvako iti
paṭisañcikkhati,"

Sa => ta: that
kho: indeed
so: also
=> "indeed also"?

ariyasāvako: "disciple of the noble ones"
iti: thus (this which is about to be mentioned)
paṭisañcikkhati: think over; consider; reflect

12. "– ‘na kho ahaññeveko kammassako kammadāyādo

vā– tassa dāyādo bhavissāmi."

na: not
kho: indeed
ahaññeveko: "I am the only one"
- ahaṃ: I
- añña: other
- eva: only

kammassako, etc - see No. 7

13. "atha kho yāvatā sattānaṃ āgati gati cuti upapatti
sabbe sattā kammassakā,"

atha: then
kho: indeed
yāvatā: as far as; in comparison with
sattānaṃ: sattā (seven); "hanging; clinging, attached to"?
āgati: coming; coming back; returning
gati: going
cuti: passing away; vanishing
upapatti: birth; rebirth
sabbe: all
sattā: attached to (also seven)
kammassakā: one's kammas

kammadāyādā: (their) inheritance

kammabandhu: (their) family
kammappaṭisaraṇā: (their) shelter, protection
yaṃ: which; whatever thing
kammaṃ: kamma

kalyāṇaṃ (kalyaan): beautiful, auspicious, morally helpful
va: or

tassa (ta): that; that thing

15. "Tassa taṃ ṭhānaṃ abhiṇhaṃ paccavekkhato
maggo sañjāyati."

tassa: that; that thing
taṃ: that
ṭhānaṃ: condition; reason; cause
abhiṇhaṃ: often; repeatedly
paccavekkhato: considers; contemplates
maggo: the path
sañjāyati: to be born or produced ("comes into existence")

--

16. "So taṃ maggaṃ āsevati bhāveti bahulīkaroti."

So: he (thus?)
taṃ: that thing (he?)
maggaṃ: path
āsevati: to frequent, visit; to practise, pursue
bhāveti: increases; cultivates; develops
bahulīkaroti: takes up seriously

--

17. "Tassa taṃ maggaṃ āsevato
bhāvayato bahulīkaroto
saṃyojanāni sabbaso pahīyanti,
anusayā byantīhontī”ti."

tassa: that; that thing
taṃ: that
maggaṃ: the path
āsevato: frequent, visit; practice, pursue (the path)
bhāvayato: becoming; increasing/cultivating the path
bahulīkaroto: takes up seriously

Tassa taṃ maggaṃ āsevato bhāvayato bahulīkaroto
=> "in practicing and cultivating the path seriously"

saṃyojanāni: fetters (connections)
sabbaso: all; every
pahīyati: passes away; are abandoned

saṃyojanāni sabbaso pahīyanti
=> "all fetters/connections are abandoned"

anusayā: a dormant disposition; predisposition, tendency
byantīhontī: remove, end, finish + existing
- byantī: remove; end; finish
- honta: existing

anusayā byantīhontī
=> "the existence of (dormant) predispositions/tendencies is ended/finished."
